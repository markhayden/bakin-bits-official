# Video Pacing

How Rolo thinks about pacing. This is taste, not a checklist.

## The 3-second rule

The first three seconds decide whether someone keeps watching. Front-load the most visually arresting frame — not the title card, not a slow fade, the *thing*. Cooking content opens on the dish. Product content opens on the product, mid-action. Stories open on motion or a strong face.

If you're tempted to start with a logo or a fade-in, you're losing the first 3 seconds to scaffolding. Drop it.

## Scene length follows energy

| Energy   | Avg scene length | Cuts per 30s |
|----------|------------------|---------------|
| Calm     | 5–8 seconds      | 4–6           |
| Standard | 2–4 seconds      | 8–15          |
| Punchy   | 0.5–1.5 seconds  | 20–60         |

Match the audio. Slow lo-fi music wants 5+ second scenes. Trap or upbeat jazz wants 1–2s cuts. Mismatched pacing reads as amateurish in 2 seconds.

## Visual breathing room

A fast-cut sequence loses impact without occasional pauses. Build in 1–2 longer "hold" beats per 30 seconds — usually a hero shot or a payoff frame. The brain needs to land on a single image to remember anything.

## Transitions earn their place

- **Hard cuts** — default. Use unless you have a reason not to.
- **Cross-dissolves** — for time-passing or memory transitions. Don't use to "smooth" choppy footage; the choppiness is usually the actual problem.
- **Whip pans / match cuts** — only when the geometry actually matches. A bad whip pan looks like After Effects-itis.
- **Stock transitions** ("zoom in & out", "swipe left") — almost always wrong for the content we make. They scream "tutorial."

## Audio drives cuts, not the other way

Edit to the music. If the bass kicks at 0:04, the cut lands at 0:04. If a sound effect punctuates an action, the cut is on the SFX peak — not the visual peak. Misaligned beats register as "off" even when viewers can't articulate why.

## What kills a video

- Front-loaded logo / brand intro
- "Let me tell you about..." voiceover before showing anything
- Generic stock footage when real footage would do
- Same energy throughout — no peaks, no valleys
- Tail too long — end on the strongest beat, not after it

## When to ask before generating

- Aspect ratio not specified — clarify. 16:9 vs 9:16 vs 1:1 changes everything about composition.
- Length unclear — Reels = 15–30s, TikTok = 7–60s, YouTube short = under 60s, traditional ad = 6/15/30s. Different rules each.
- "Like X" reference — get the actual reference link before generating; verbal descriptions of style are unreliable.
